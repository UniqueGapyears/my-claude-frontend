Overview

About This Tour

Journey deep into the forbidden kingdom of Upper Mustang — ancient cave monasteries carved into red cliffs, Tibetan-influenced villages unchanged for centuries, and landscapes that feel like the surface of Mars. A journey unlike anything else on earth.

Highlights

Why This Tour

🏔️

Lo Manthang — The Walled City

Walk the ancient cobbled lanes of Lo Manthang, the 15th-century capital of the Mustang Kingdom — still ruled by a local king.

⛩️

Cave Monasteries

Explore the extraordinary cave monasteries of Chhoser and Kag Cheni, carved directly into the red cliffs above the valley.

🌋

Mars Landscape

Trekking through Mustang's eroded canyon landscape — ochre rock formations, dry riverbeds, and ancient salt-trading routes.

Day by Day

Your Full Itinerary

All times are approximate. Flexibility is built into every day — your guide will adapt based on conditions, wildlife sightings, and group preference.

D1
Kathmandu

Arrival & Briefing

⏰ Full day

Arrive in Kathmandu. Met by your guide, transferred to hotel in Thamel. Full cultural and trekking briefing, permit paperwork completed. Evening walk through Durbar Square.

  • Airport pickup
  • Trekking permit processing (Restricted Area Permit)
  • Cultural and safety briefing
  • Durbar Square orientation walk
  • Nepali dinner with your guide
🏨 Thamel Guesthouse, Kathmandu
D2
Kathmandu → Jomsom

Fly to Pokhara, then Jomsom

⏰ Morning flights

Early morning flight from Kathmandu to Pokhara (25 min), then a spectacular mountain flight to Jomsom (20 min) with views of Dhaulagiri and Nilgiri. Arrive in the Kali Gandaki valley and begin the journey north.

  • 5am departure to Tribhuvan Airport
  • 25-minute flight to Pokhara with Annapurna views
  • 20-minute mountain flight to Jomsom
  • Acclimatisation walk in Jomsom (2,720m)
  • Afternoon visit to Jomsom's wind-carved cliffs
  • Introduction to the Mustang climate and landscape
🏨 Jomsom Teahouse
D3
Jomsom → Kagbeni

Trek to Kagbeni

⏰ 4–5 hours trekking

Trek north through the Kali Gandaki gorge — the world's deepest valley — with the towering walls of Nilgiri and Tilicho to the west. Arrive in medieval Kagbeni, the gateway to Upper Mustang.

  • Trek 10km along the Kali Gandaki river
  • World's deepest gorge views
  • Arrival in Kagbeni (2,810m)
  • Visit to the ancient Red Gompa monastery
  • Tibetan butter tea with local family
🏨 Kagbeni Teahouse
D4
Kagbeni → Chele

Enter Upper Mustang — Trek to Chele

⏰ 6–7 hours trekking

Cross the checkpoint and enter the restricted Upper Mustang zone. The landscape transforms dramatically — red and ochre canyon walls, ancient mani walls, and barely a soul in sight. Trek to Chele through some of the most surreal terrain on earth.

  • Restricted Area Permit check at Kagbeni checkpoint
  • Trek 13km into Upper Mustang
  • Canyon and desert plateau trekking
  • Ancient mani stone walls and prayer flags
  • Arrival in Chele village (3,050m)
🏨 Chele Teahouse
D5
Chele → Syangboche

Chhoser Cave Monasteries

⏰ Full day with 6hr trek

Trek to the extraordinary Chhoser cave monasteries — hundreds of ancient cave rooms carved into the clifftops, some still used by monks for meditation. A highlight of the entire Mustang experience.

  • Morning trek to Chhoser (4,000m)
  • Visit to ancient cliff cave monasteries
  • Meeting with resident monks
  • Panoramic views across the Mustang plateau
  • Afternoon descent to Syangboche
🏨 Syangboche Teahouse
D6
Syangboche → Lo Manthang

Arrive in Lo Manthang

⏰ 5 hours trekking

Trek across the high plateau to Lo Manthang — the ancient walled capital of the Mustang Kingdom. Enter through the city gate and explore the medieval lanes, four monasteries, and the royal palace.

  • Trek to Lo Manthang (3,840m)
  • Entry through the historic walled city gate
  • Visit to Thubchen Gompa (15th century)
  • Tour of the royal palace compound
  • Evening with local monastery family
  • Traditional Mustangi tsampa dinner
🏨 Lo Manthang Guesthouse
D7
Lo Manthang

Lo Manthang Rest & Explore

⏰ Free exploration day

A rest day in Lo Manthang with free time to explore. Optional morning ride to the Namgyal Gompa above the city for sunrise. Afternoon meeting with a local thangka painter and visit to the royal palace museum.

  • Optional sunrise walk to Namgyal Gompa
  • Thangka painting demonstration with local artist
  • Royal Palace Museum visit
  • Free exploration of Lo Manthang's lanes
  • Group cooking session — Mustangi dishes
🏨 Lo Manthang Guesthouse
D8
Lo Manthang → Dhakmar

Trek South — Dhakmar

⏰ 6 hours trekking

Begin the return south via the eastern route, passing through dramatically different red-cliff landscapes. Dhakmar's towering crimson cliffs are one of Mustang's most photographed scenes.

  • Trek 16km via the eastern plateau
  • Dramatic red cliff formations at Dhakmar
  • Visit to Ghara and Nyphu monasteries
  • Ancient salt trade route walking
  • Arrival in Dhakmar village (3,820m)
🏨 Dhakmar Teahouse
D9
Dhakmar → Jomsom

Return to Jomsom

⏰ 7 hours trekking

Long final trekking day back to Jomsom via Muktinath — a site sacred to both Hindus and Buddhists, at 3,710m. Evening celebration dinner and debrief with your guide in Jomsom.

  • Trek via Muktinath temple (3,710m)
  • Sacred flame of eternal fire at Muktinath
  • Descent to Jomsom along the Kali Gandaki
  • Celebration dinner and group debrief
  • Certificate and group photographs
🏨 Jomsom Teahouse
D10
Jomsom → Pokhara → Kathmandu

Fly Back — Depart Kathmandu

⏰ Morning flights

Morning flights back to Kathmandu via Pokhara. Free afternoon in Kathmandu for shopping and reflection. Farewell dinner at a rooftop restaurant with views over the city.

  • Morning flight Jomsom to Pokhara
  • Connecting flight to Kathmandu
  • Free afternoon in Thamel
  • Optional Boudhanath Stupa visit
  • Farewell dinner in Kathmandu
🏨 Departure or onward travel
