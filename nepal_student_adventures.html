The Hidden Corners

Nepal Beyond the Guidebook

These are the places, people and traditions most travellers never reach — and the ones our students talk about for years afterwards.

🦏

Chitwan's Inner Jungle

Beyond the resort safaris, deep in Chitwan with Tharu trackers — where rhino, gharial and Bengal tigers still roam.

🏔️

Upper Mustang

The forbidden kingdom — ancient cave monasteries, eroded canyon landscapes and Tibetan Buddhist culture largely unchanged for centuries.

🏘️

Remote Annapurna Villages

Ghalegaun, Sikles, Ghaletar — settlements that see a fraction of the traffic of the main circuit, with extraordinary highland hospitality.

💎

Shey Phoksundo Lake

One of the most spectacular alpine lakes on earth, deep in Dolpo — turquoise waters surrounded by thousand-metre cliffs in near-total silence.

🏛️

Bandipur Old Town

A perfectly preserved hilltop Newari town that feels frozen in the 19th century — cobbled streets, traditional courtyards, and no through traffic.

🌄

Gorkha Royal Palace

The ancestral home of Nepal's royal dynasty — a stunning hilltop palace above the clouds, visited by barely a handful of tourists each week.

🛶

Koshi Tappu Wetlands

Nepal's oldest wildlife reserve — a birdwatcher's paradise in the eastern Terai, home to wild water buffalo and hundreds of migratory species.

⛩️

Palpa & Tansen

A hilltop town of Newari metalworkers, forgotten Buddhist shrines, and some of the most spectacular valley views in the entire country.

Responsible Travel

We Leave Places
Better

Our commitment to responsible tourism is not a marketing line — it is built into every booking, every itinerary, and every relationship we have with local communities.

Book Responsibly
🏡

Community Lodges

We stay in community-owned accommodation wherever possible, ensuring tourism income stays in the village.

👨‍🏫

Local Expert Guides

Every guide is a trained local — from the same region or community we visit. No outside operators.

🌱

Low-Impact Routes

We avoid overcrowded trails, use clean transport where available, and carry out all waste from remote areas.

📖

Cultural Briefings

Students receive in-depth cultural and historical briefings before every experience, so engagement is respectful and informed.

💷

Fair Pay

Every porter, cook, guide and host is paid above the local living wage. We publish our pay rates on request.

🦁

Wildlife Ethics

No elephant rides. No captive animal encounters. Wildlife experiences are always observation-only with trained naturalists.
