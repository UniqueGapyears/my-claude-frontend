Overview

About This Tour

Explore the Okavango Delta by mokoro canoe, on foot, and by 4x4 through one of Africa's last true wildernesses — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the greatest wildlife destinations on earth.

Highlights

Why This Tour

🛶

Mokoro Canoe Safari

Gliding silently through the papyrus channels of the Okavango in a traditional dugout canoe — the most intimate wildlife encounter in Africa.

🐘

Big Five on Foot

Walking safaris with an armed guide in the Okavango — elephant, lion, wild dog, and leopard all regularly sighted in this concentrated wilderness.

🌍

UNESCO World Heritage

The Okavango Delta is the world's largest inland delta — a unique ecosystem where the desert meets a vast, ever-shifting waterway teeming with life.

Day by Day

Your Full Itinerary

All times approximate. Your guide adapts each day to conditions, wildlife, and group preference.

D1
Maun, Botswana

Arrive Maun — Delta Briefing

⏰ Day 1

Arrive at Maun International Airport — the gateway to the Okavango. Full expedition briefing, gear check, and an evening drive to the Delta edge for sunset.

  • Airport pickup in Maun
  • Expedition briefing — ecology, safety, wildlife
  • Gear check and camp introduction
  • Delta edge sunset drive
  • Welcome dinner under the stars
🏨 Maun Guesthouse
D2
Maun → Delta Camp

Light Aircraft — Into the Delta

⏰ Day 2 — Morning flight

The unforgettable way to enter the Okavango — a 30-minute light aircraft flight over the Delta's papyrus channels, elephant pathways, and hippo pools. Land on a bush airstrip and transfer to camp by mokoro.

  • 6am: departure from Maun airstrip
  • 30-minute Cessna flight over the Okavango
  • Aerial views of the Delta waterways
  • Land on remote bush airstrip
  • Mokoro canoe transfer to camp
  • Afternoon first game walk around camp
🏨 Delta Tented Camp
D3
Okavango Delta channels

Mokoro Canoe Days

⏰ Days 3–4 — On the water

Two days exploring the Delta channels by mokoro — the traditional dugout canoe poled by your expert guide. Silent, close to the water, and extraordinary for wildlife and birds at every turn.

  • 5:30am: dawn mokoro departure
  • Papyrus channel navigation
  • Hippo observation (from safe distance)
  • Water lily forest exploration
  • Sitatunga sighting (rare water antelope)
  • Bird-rich islands and floodplain walks
  • Day 4: extended channel exploration — fishing eagles and bee-eaters
🏨 Delta Tented Camp
D4
Okavango islands

Walking Safari Days

⏰ Days 5–6 — On foot

Two days of walking safaris on the Delta's islands — approaching wildlife on foot with your armed guide. Elephant encounters at close range, lion tracking, and wild dog sightings (one of the world's most endangered predators).

  • 5:30am: dawn walking safari
  • Elephant herd approach on foot
  • Lion spoor tracking
  • African wild dog territory (seasonal)
  • Buffalo herd observation
  • Leopard tracking with expert guide
  • Evening campfire under extraordinary skies
🏨 Delta Tented Camp
D5
Moremi Game Reserve

4x4 Game Drive — Moremi

⏰ Day 7

Full-day 4x4 game drive into Moremi Game Reserve — the most wildlife-dense section of the Okavango system. Famous for predator sightings — lion, cheetah, and leopard all regularly encountered.

  • Full-day 4x4 game drive into Moremi
  • Big cat territory — lion and cheetah tracking
  • Extensive buffalo and elephant herds
  • Xakanaxa Lagoon hippo and crocodile
  • Return to camp for final night celebration dinner
🏨 Delta Tented Camp
D6
Delta → Maun

Final Morning & Fly Out

⏰ Day 8

Final dawn mokoro before breakfast, then the light aircraft back to Maun. Afternoon at leisure in Maun before onward flights.

  • Dawn final mokoro canal
  • Breakfast at camp — final Delta morning
  • Light aircraft flight back to Maun
  • Afternoon free in Maun
  • Onward international flights
